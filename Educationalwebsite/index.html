What is Child Abuse?

Child abuse is defined as any action that causes significant harm to a child. This can occur in various forms, including physical, emotional, sexual, and neglectful behaviors. Understanding child abuse is crucial for everyone—children, teens, and adults—to help protect vulnerable individuals.

Abuse can manifest in many ways, and recognizing its signs is essential for prevention. It may occur in a variety of environments: at home, in schools, or within the community. It's important to foster open conversations about safety and well-being in families, schools, and communities.

Child abuse is a serious issue that affects millions of children worldwide. According to the World Health Organization, approximately 1 in 4 adults report having been physically abused as children, which highlights the need for increased awareness and preventive measures.
